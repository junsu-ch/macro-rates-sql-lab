# Financial Concepts

This file explains the market concepts used in the project.

## 2s10s Curve

The 2s10s curve is calculated as:

10-year Treasury yield - 2-year Treasury yield

If the spread is positive, the curve is upward sloping.

If the spread is negative, the curve is inverted.

## Why the 2Y Yield Matters

The 2-year Treasury yield is strongly influenced by expectations for central bank policy over the next few years.

If investors expect the central bank to cut rates, the 2Y yield usually falls.

If investors expect the central bank to keep rates high or hike more, the 2Y yield usually rises.

## Why the 10Y Yield Matters

The 10-year Treasury yield reflects several forces:

- expected future short-term rates
- inflation expectations
- growth expectations
- term premium
- supply and demand for long-duration bonds

## Why Traders Watch the Curve

The yield curve helps traders understand how the market is pricing monetary policy, growth, inflation, and recession risk.

A deeply inverted curve often suggests that short-term policy rates are high relative to longer-term growth and inflation expectations.
